How many Google reviews does an HVAC company need to rank? We scraped it.

AP By Aaron Phillips · Booked Job · Updated June 2026
Short answer: We scraped Google Business Profiles across major Texas metros. The HVAC contractors ranking on page 1 of Google Maps have a median of 519 reviews (Booked Job GBP scrape, 2026). The short answer

Top-ranked HVAC companies have a median of 519 Google reviews.

Our own scrape of Google Business Profiles found that the HVAC contractors sitting on page 1 of Google Maps in major Texas metros have a median of 519 reviews (Booked Job GBP scrape, 2026). That is the number to beat. It is also the highest review bar of any trade we measured, by a wide margin.

Why 519 is so much higher than other trades

HVAC has the steepest review bar of any trade we scraped.

Here is the full review bar from our scrape, ranked highest to lowest (Booked Job GBP scrape, 2026): HVAC 519, Plumbing 337, Roofing 144, Painting 109, Electrical 64.

HVAC needs more than three times the reviews that painters need, and more than eight times what electricians need. Why? Lots of HVAC companies fight for the same towns, and homeowners call a lot when the AC dies in a Texas summer. More jobs, more competitors, more reviews. The bar climbs.

This is the non-obvious part: the review bar is not a trophy, it is the price of entry. Hitting 519 does not put you on top. It gets you into the room with the contractors who already rank. Below it, you are mostly invisible on the map.

Reviews are the thing buyers actually look at

Almost everyone reads reviews before they pick a contractor.

This bar matters because reviews are how people decide. 91% of people read local reviews, and most won't even consider a business under 4 stars (BrightLocal 2025). 81% rely on Google reviews specifically (CallRail 2026).

So two things have to be true. You need enough reviews to clear the bar and rank, and you need the stars high enough that people don't scroll past you. A pile of reviews with a 3.6 average works against you. Volume and rating both count.

Responding to reviews is free leverage

Replying to every review makes more buyers favor you.

Most contractors get this wrong. 88% of people would use a business that responds to all its reviews (BrightLocal 2024). That is a free edge. It costs nothing but a few minutes, and most of your competitors aren't doing it.

Reply to the good ones with a thank-you. Reply to the bad ones calm and professional. People reading later see how you handle a problem, and that often matters more than the complaint itself.

How to actually get to 519

Ask every happy customer, every time, the same day.

There is no trick. You get reviews by asking, and by asking consistently. The companies at 519 didn't get there by accident. They built a habit: finish the job, ask for the review while the customer is happy, send a direct link to their Google profile.

Do the math on your own pace. If a tech closes out a handful of jobs a day and even a fraction leave a review, you build steadily over months. The contractors ahead of you have been doing this for years. The only way to catch up is to start the habit now and never drop it.

Reviews protect the leads you already pay for

Reviews lift the return on every dollar you spend getting found.

HVAC already has strong paid economics. On Google Local Services Ads, HVAC runs about a $51 cost per lead, 44% of those leads book, an average ticket around $2,110, and a 9.55x return on ad spend (SearchLight 2026).

But ads only put you in front of people. Reviews are what make them pick you. When someone sees your ad or your map listing and then sees 500-plus reviews at a strong rating, they trust you before they call. Your reviews and your ad spend work together. Weak reviews waste the clicks you paid for.
