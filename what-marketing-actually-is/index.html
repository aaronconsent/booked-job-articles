What Marketing Actually Is for Contractors

AP By Aaron Phillips · Marketing 101 · Lesson 1 of 10 · Updated June 2026
Short answer: Marketing isn't ads or a logo. It's a machine that takes a stranger with a problem and turns them into a paid job: get found, get picked, get booked. Everything else is decoration.

Let's get one thing straight before we start. You didn't get into this trade to become a marketer. You got in to do good work, get paid, and go home. So when some guy in a vest shows up talking about "brand synergy" and "engagement," your nonsense detector goes off. Good. It should. Most of what gets sold to you as marketing is decoration — pretty logos and cute little posts that nobody ever booked a job from. We're not doing that here.

The Real Definition

GET FOUND → GET PICKED → GET BOOKED

Here's what marketing actually is, with the crap scraped off. Marketing is the machine that takes a stranger with a problem — a busted water heater, a leaking roof, a dead AC in the middle of July — and turns them into a paid job on your calendar. That's the whole thing. Get found. Get picked. Get booked. Everything else is wallpaper.

The Only Scoreboard

likes ❌ followers ❌ BOOKED JOBS ✅

Notice what I didn't say. I didn't say go viral. I didn't say likes. I didn't say "brand awareness." You can't cash a like. You can't deposit a follower. The only scoreboard that pays your guys on Friday is booked jobs. That's literally why this is called Booked. I don't care if you're famous. I care if your calendar's full.

You'Re A Buyer, Not A Marketer

You don't need to be a marketer. You need to be impossible to fleece.

Now here's the good news, and it's the most important thing I'll say all day. You do not have to become a marketing genius. You'll never out-nerd the agencies, and you don't need to. You just need to know enough to not get fleeced. Because right now, if a marketer walks in and starts slinging words at you, you've got two moves: nod along and hope, or hand over a card and pray. Both of those end with you broke and fed up. By the time we're done, you'll know which questions to ask, which numbers to demand, and exactly when somebody's blowing smoke.

The Two Numbers (Sets Up Course 2)

CPL vs CPBJ · "Cheap is expensive.

And it all comes down to two numbers. What it costs you to get a lead — and what it costs you to get a booked job. Those are not the same number, and mixing them up is exactly how contractors go broke buying "cheap" leads. So write this on the wall: a cheap lead that never books is the most expensive lead there is. You'll pay twenty bucks for a lead that ghosts you and feel like a genius. Meanwhile the guy paying eighty for a lead that actually books is eating your lunch. We're gonna fix that thinking. That's the very next course — Know Your Numbers — and it's the most important one in here.

The Five Doors

5 DOORS — Google/Map · Website · Paid Ads · Lead Networks · Reviews

So here's where we're headed. First we figure out where customers actually find you — and there are really only five doors they come through. Your Google profile and the map. Your website. Paid ads. Lead networks like Angi and Thumbtack. And your reviews. Some of those doors are free. Some of them cost real money. And most contractors ignore the free ones and overpay for the rest. Backwards.

Door By Door (The Rest Of The Course)

GBP · Website · Reviews · Paid Leads · Paid Ads · Google + AI · Scorecard

Then we go door by door. We claim and fix the most valuable free thing you own — your Google Business Profile — because that little map at the top of the search beats everybody sitting under it. We tackle your website, which has exactly one job: turn a visitor into a phone call. Most contractor sites convert 2–3%. Ninety-eight out of a hundred people show up and leave like ghosts. Pretty doesn't pay. Working pays. We get into reviews, because your reputation is straight-up currency now — nine out of ten people read 'em before they ever call you, and most won't even consider you under four stars. We get honest about paid leads — when they're worth it, and when they're a trap. We spend your first ad dollar without lighting it on fire. We get you found by Google *and* by AI, because about a third of people now start with a chatbot instead of a search bar. And we finish with your scorecard — the handful of numbers you check every month so you're the one directing your marketing, instead of getting robbed by it.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to be good at marketing to get more jobs?

No. You need to understand it well enough to make smart calls and not get fleeced — get found, get picked, get booked. That's it.

Is marketing the same as advertising?

No. Ads are one small piece. Marketing is being findable and trustable the moment someone needs your trade — most of which is free.

What's the one metric that actually matters?

Booked jobs. Not likes, not followers, not 'brand awareness.' If it doesn't fill your calendar, it doesn't count.
