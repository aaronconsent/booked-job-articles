The 5 Doors Every Customer Comes Through

AP By Aaron Phillips · Marketing 101 · Lesson 3 of 10 · Updated June 2026
Short answer: Customers come through five doors: your Google profile, your website, paid ads, lead apps, and reviews. Two are free and come first. Living behind one door is a time bomb.

Customers don't find you by magic. They don't stumble onto your truck and feel a connection. They come through doors. And here's the thing nobody tells you — there are only five of them. Five doors, that's it. Once you can see all five, you stop throwing money at random and start running this like a business.

5
doors — not fifty. Google/map, website, paid ads, lead apps, reviews. Most contractors use one or two and leave the rest wide open for competitors.

The Five Doors

5 DOORS — Google · Website · Ads · Lead Apps · Reviews

So here they are. Door one, your Google profile and the map — that little pack at the top of the search. Door two, your website. Door three, paid ads. Door four, lead networks, the apps like Angi and Thumbtack. And door five, your reviews and word of mouth. Every single customer you will ever book walks through one of those five. Memorize them.

Free Foundation Vs Paid Accelerator

FREE foundation → THEN paid accelerator

Now, two of those doors are free. Your Google profile and your reviews don't cost you a dime, and they're the foundation everything else sits on. The other three — ads, lead apps, paid placement — those cost real money. And here's where most guys get it wrong. They ignore the free doors, the ones that actually compound over time, and dump their whole budget into the paid ones. That's building your house starting with the roof.

The Healthy Mix

40% paid · 30% Google/site · 20% referral · 10% other

A healthy contractor doesn't live behind one door. Picture a pie. Maybe forty percent of your work comes from paid — ads and lead apps. Thirty percent from Google and your website. Twenty percent from referrals and repeat customers, the gold. And ten percent from everything else. No single door owns you. That's a business that sleeps at night.

All Eggs, One Basket

100% one app = a TIME BOMB

Now here's the nightmare. The guy who gets a hundred percent of his work from one app. One door. He's not a business owner — he's a renter. The day that app jacks its prices, or changes the rules, or just shuts his account off with no warning, his phone goes dead silent. I've watched it happen. One door is not a strategy. It's a time bomb.

Different Trade, Different Doors

emergency → search/ads · big projects → reviews/site

And which doors matter most depends on your trade. Emergency work — plumbing, HVAC, a busted anything — that lives and dies on Google and ads, because people search the second it breaks. Bigger project work — roofing, remodels, a new system — that leans harder on reviews and a real website, because folks research before they spend big. Same five doors. Different weight.

Do This Now

CIRCLE THE DOORS YOU USE

Here's your homework. Five doors. Circle the ones you actually use right now. Be honest. Most owners circle one, maybe two, and leave the other three wide open for the competition to walk through. The doors you're not using aren't closed — somebody else is standing in them, taking your jobs.

Frequently asked questions

What are the main ways customers find a contractor?

Five channels: Google Business Profile and the map pack, your website, paid ads, lead networks like Angi/Thumbtack, and reviews / word of mouth.

Which marketing channels are free for contractors?

Your Google Business Profile and your reviews cost nothing and are the foundation everything else sits on. Build those before paying for ads or leads.

Is it bad to get all my leads from one app?

Yes. One channel is a time bomb — the day that app raises prices or shuts your account off, your phone goes silent. Spread across doors.
