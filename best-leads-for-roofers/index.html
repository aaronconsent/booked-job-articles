The short answer

Roofing leads cost more than almost any trade — and that is exactly why they pay.

A roofing lead from Google Search runs about $79, per LocaliQ 2025 — the most expensive trade they tracked except general contracting at ~$94. Roofing websites turn 3-7% of visitors into customers, per WebFX 2026. High cost, low convert. The fix is speed and follow-up, not cheaper leads.

Why roofing leads cost more than HVAC or plumbing

Storm-driven demand and big tickets push roofing to the top of the price chart.

Look at Google Search cost per lead by trade, per LocaliQ 2025: HVAC ~$45, plumbing ~$52, electrical ~$58, then roofing jumps to ~$79. Only general contracting costs more at ~$94. The average click across trades runs $6.59.

Roofing sits high because demand is lumpy. A hailstorm rolls through and every homeowner in the zip code searches "roof repair" the same week. More contractors bid for those clicks, so the price climbs. You are not overpaying — you are paying storm-season prices on a storm-season trade.

3-7% convert — and that is normal

Most people who land on your roofing site leave without calling, and that is true for the whole trade.

Roofing websites convert 3-7% of visitors, per WebFX 2026 — same band as HVAC and remodeling. Compare that to plumbing at 12-16%. Plumbing is an emergency; roofing is a research-and-quote decision. People shop. They get three bids. That gap is built into the job, not a sign your site is broken.

For context, the average contractor website converts just 2-3% overall, and about 98% of visitors leave without contacting anyone, per WebFX 2026. So 3-7% is already above the floor.

Speed is the cheapest upgrade you have

The first roofer to call back usually wins the job before the others even see the lead.

78% of homeowners hire the first contractor who responds, per Lead Connect 2026. And a 5-minute callback makes you 100x more likely to qualify the lead than waiting 30 minutes, per MIT Sloan 2026.

That is the whole game with paid leads. You bought the click at $79. If you let it sit an hour, you handed it to the next roofer for free. Worse: contractors miss 14% of their inbound calls, per CallRail 2026. Every missed call on a paid roofing lead is money set on fire.

Reviews decide who even gets the call

Homeowners filter out low-star roofers before they ever pick up the phone.

91% of people read local reviews and most won't consider a business under 4 stars, per BrightLocal 2025. 81% lean on Google reviews specifically, and 88% would use a business that responds to all its reviews (BrightLocal 2024).

There is also a review-count bar to clear. Roofers ranking on page one of Google Maps in major Texas metros have a median of 144 Google reviews, per Booked Job's 2026 GBP scrape. That is lower than HVAC (519) or plumbing (337) — so roofing is a more winnable map than most trades. Get to ~144 honest reviews and you are in the race.

How to make paid roofing leads pay

Treat the $79 lead like cash and the math works; treat it like a tipsheet and you lose.

Three moves. One: answer fast — 5 minutes beats 30 by 100x, per MIT Sloan 2026, and missed calls cost you 14% of inbound, per CallRail 2026. Two: stack reviews to the page-one roofing bar of ~144, per Booked Job 2026, so buyers pick you. Three: own more of your own demand — owned SEO runs about $290-310 per booked job, per 2026 lead-network comparisons, so over time you lean less on the priciest clicks.
