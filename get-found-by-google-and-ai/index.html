Get Found by Google AND AI

AP By Aaron Phillips · Marketing 101 · Lesson 9 of 10 · Updated June 2026
Short answer: Getting found used to mean Google. Now a third of people ask an AI tool who to hire. The same foundation — a strong profile, real reviews, a clear website that answers questions — wins both.

For twenty years, getting found meant one thing — show up on Google when somebody types plumber near me. That's still true. But there's a new front door now, and it's growing fast. People are straight-up asking a chatbot who to hire. So today, we get you found in both places — the search bar, and the robot.

Seo In Plain English

SEO = show up free for "[trade] near me

First, the old reliable. S-E-O. Forget the jargon — it just means showing up when somebody searches your trade in your town, without paying for the click. It's slow. It compounds. It's basically free. Think of it like a tree — you plant it, nothing happens for a while, and then one day it's shading your whole yard with free leads.

The New Door: Ai

AEO = get named when they ask the AI

Now the new one. A-E-O — answer engine optimization. Fancy name, simple idea. About a third of people now start with an AI tool instead of a search bar. They type, who's the best plumber in my area, and the robot just hands them a couple of names. You want to be one of those names. If you're not, you're invisible to a third of your market and you don't even know it.

Who The Ai Names

clear pages + reviews + everywhere = the AI names you

So how does the robot pick? Same way a smart customer would. It reads the web and looks for the business that shows up everywhere, says clearly what it does, and has the reviews to back it up. The contractor with a strong Google profile, a clear website, and a pile of recent reviews gets named. The ghost with a blank profile does not. The AI isn't magic — it's just repeating what the internet already says about you.

One Foundation Feeds Both

same foundation → ranks on Google AND named by AI

And here's the beautiful part — you've already been building this. The exact same stuff that wins on Google wins with the A I. A claimed profile. Real reviews. A clear website that says what you do and where you do it. You don't need two strategies. You need one solid foundation, and it feeds both doors at the same time.

Content That Answers Questions

answer real questions → Google + AI quote you

One more lever. Answer the questions people actually ask. How much does a water heater cost to replace. Why is my A C freezing up. Put real, plain answers on your site. Google rewards it, the AI quotes it, and the customer trusts you before they ever call. Be the one who answered the question, not the one who hid the price.

Do This Now

Google yourself + ASK the AI — where do you rank?

Homework, takes two minutes. Pull out your phone. Google your trade plus your town — see where you actually rank. Then open an AI tool and ask it who the best in your area is. See if your name comes up. Wherever you're not showing up — that's your map. That's exactly where the work is.

Frequently asked questions

How do I get my business recommended by ChatGPT or AI?

The AI repeats what the web says about you. Get named by having a strong Google profile, a clear website that says exactly what you do and where, and a pile of recent reviews.

What is AEO (answer engine optimization)?

It's showing up when people ask an AI tool a question instead of searching. Same foundations as SEO — clear pages, real content, reviews, citations.

Do I need a separate strategy for AI search?

No. The exact same foundation that ranks you on Google gets you named by the AI. Build it once; it feeds both doors.
